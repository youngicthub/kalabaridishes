About Kalabari Culture

The Kalabari people are an ethnic group in Rivers State, Nigeria, known for their rich cultural heritage. Here are some key aspects of Kalabari culture:

  • Language: The Kalabari people speak the Kalabari language, which is part of the Ijaw language group. It is spoken by the people in the Kalabari Kingdom and its environs.
  • Traditional Religion: Traditionally, the Kalabari people practiced a form of African traditional religion, which involved the worship of various deities and spirits. However, due to the influence of Christianity and Islam, many Kalabari people have converted to these religions.
  • Art and Craft: The Kalabari people are known for their exquisite art and craft, particularly in the areas of wood carving, pottery, and beadwork. Their art often reflects their cultural beliefs and practices.

Our Foods

Kalabari cuisine is known for its rich and flavorful dishes, often incorporating a variety of seafood, meats, and vegetables. Here are some popular Kalabari dishes:

Ofe Oghoni

This is a traditional Kalabari seafood stew made with a variety of seafood such as fish, prawns, and crab, cooked in a rich and spicy tomato-based sauce.

Abala

Abala is a steamed bean pudding made from black-eyed peas. It is usually wrapped in banana leaves and steamed until cooked. It is often served with a spicy pepper sauce.

Oto

Oto is a mashed yam dish that is often served as a side dish. It is made by boiling yam until soft, then mashing it and seasoning it with palm oil, onions, and spices.

Kekefia

Kekefia is a traditional Kalabari dish made from shredded plantains mixed with spices and then deep-fried until crispy. It is often served as a snack or side dish.

Pepper Soup

Pepper soup is a popular Nigerian dish, and the Kalabari version often includes fish or meat cooked in a spicy broth with peppers and other spices.
